# Timeline

## Phase 1 — Pre-Launch Research

Researchers develop technology capable of transmitting emotional states between individuals.

Early experiments generate both excitement and ethical concern.

Because the work depends partly on fine-grained first-person reporting, some contemplative practitioners become adjacent to the research process.

---

## Phase 2 — Sangha Involvement and Debate

A contemplative community begins discussing the spiritual implications of empathy technology.

One or more Sangha members become involved in early Empathy Engine phenomenology and calibration work.

This gives the Sangha unusually direct visibility into the technology's underlying assumptions.

Members worry that machine-mediated empathy may violate the integrity of human consciousness.

A specific warning emerges from inside the community:

if emotional states can be captured, they can be retained, replayed, detached from relationship, and eventually exploited.

---

## Phase 3 — The Retained Session

A deeply disturbing pre-launch incident involving empathy technology occurs.

A respected Sangha member involved in the research discovers that recorded affective material taken from them has been retained and reused after being treated as temporary.

For the Sangha, this is the first proof that the technology is already converting inner life into reusable infrastructure.

The member dies not long afterward.

The death is a random mugging gone wrong, unrelated to the research in direct causal terms.

Their death transforms ethical concern into communal grief and irreversible moral rupture.

The event fractures the community.

Some members believe the technology must be stopped.

---

## Phase 4 — Formation of HumansRHumans

A faction breaks away and forms HumansRHumans.

This happens before the opening scenes of *What It Feels Like to Be You*.

Early members know the teacher died in unrelated street violence.

Even so, the movement's earliest public language allows the death to fuse with the moral case against empathy technology.

This becomes the movement's first rhetorical compromise.

The movement begins as a morally serious resistance group.

Early goals include:

- raising awareness
- slowing deployment
- pushing for regulation

---

## Phase 5 — Internet Expansion

The movement spreads online.

By the time the Empathy Engine's first successful human trial and public announcement occur in *What It Feels Like to Be You*, HumansRHumans already exists in early form.

The public launch accelerates the movement rather than creating it.

New members join who were never part of the original Sangha.

The ideology becomes simplified and amplified.

---

## Phase 6 — Fragmentation

Cells form around the world.

Different factions interpret the ideology differently.

Some remain nonviolent.

Others escalate.

---

## Phase 7 — Regulation of Empathy Technology

Governments impose strict regulation.

The original political goal of HumansRHumans is largely achieved.

---

## Phase 8 — Ideological Persistence

Despite regulation, the movement continues.

Some factions claim regulation is insufficient.

The ideology intensifies.

The movement begins to fracture into extreme splinter groups.
