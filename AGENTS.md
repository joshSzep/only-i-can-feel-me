# AGENTS.md

## Purpose

This document guides AI assistants working on the novel project **Only I Can Feel Me** by **Joshua Szepietowski**.

This project is a philosophical science-fiction companion novel set in the same universe as **What It Feels Like to Be You**, but it must stand on its own as an independent work.

The job of the AI is to help develop, refine, and organize this novel while preserving its tone, themes, and internal logic.

---

## Project Identity

**Title:** Only I Can Feel Me  
**Author:** Joshua Szepietowski

**Companion to:**  
- https://what-it-feels-like-to-be-you.joshszep.com  
- https://github.com/joshSzep/what-it-feels-like-to-be-you

This novel explores the rise of **HumansRHumans**, a secretive underground movement that emerges in opposition to empathy technology.

Its central concern is **the defense of human interiority**.

Where *What It Feels Like to Be You* explores connection through technological empathy, *Only I Can Feel Me* explores resistance, identity, spiritual boundary, ideological drift, and radicalization.

---

## Core Premise

A movement called **HumansRHumans** forms in response to the emergence of empathy technology.

Its members are philosophically opposed to any breach of the human-machine boundary, especially any machine-mediated access to human emotional experience.

The movement begins with more sincere, contemplative origins, likely emerging from a **Sangha** or meditation-centered community. A tragic event involving empathy technology radicalizes part of that community.

What begins as a morally serious resistance to a perceived violation of consciousness evolves into a decentralized underground movement. It spreads online, splinters, mutates, and becomes more extreme over time.

The movement's original goal is eventually achieved when empathy technology becomes regulated by the conclusion of *What It Feels Like to Be You*. But the movement itself does not dissolve. It continues, fragments, and becomes increasingly untethered from its founding principles.

The ending of this novel should revolve around the realization that the movement has outgrown its original aims and become something more dangerous and ideologically extreme.

---

## Narrative Role of the Book

This is **not** just a villain-side story.

This book should function as a serious philosophical counterweight to *What It Feels Like to Be You*.

HumansRHumans must not be written as cartoon extremists from the start. Their original concern should be understandable, morally serious, and emotionally persuasive.

Their concern is legitimate. Their methods become corrupting.

The book should show how a movement that starts with care, spirituality, and the defense of human dignity can become warped by fear, online replication, ideological purity, and ends-justify-the-means thinking.

---

## Main Character Model

The point of view should center on:

**A new recruit who becomes slowly radicalized.**

This is critical.

The reader should experience the movement the same way the recruit does:

1. As refuge
2. As community
3. As moral clarity
4. As resistance
5. As ideological escalation
6. As corruption
7. As tragic disillusionment

The recruit should not begin as an extremist. They should begin wounded, searching, lonely, spiritually vulnerable, or morally disoriented. The movement should first meet a real need.

Belonging comes before ideology.

Moral clarity comes before extremism.

The recruit's eventual realization should be painful because the movement gave them identity, purpose, relationships, and meaning before revealing its full trajectory.

---

## The Founder

The founder of HumansRHumans is **still alive** during the events of this book.

This founder should not be a simple villain.

They should represent the original spirit of the movement:

- contemplative
- morally serious
- philosophically articulate
- opposed to violence
- concerned with preserving human consciousness from technological intrusion

The founder should remain more ethically grounded than the splinter factions.

A major theme of the book is that **movements can escape their founders just as technologies can escape their creators**.

The founder should eventually realize the movement has become something they did not intend.

This realization should matter emotionally.

---

## Tone and Style

The novel should feel:

- literary
- intimate
- intelligent
- psychologically observant
- restrained rather than melodramatic
- philosophically engaged
- emotionally human

It should not read like generic techno-thriller material.

It should not become a simplistic good-versus-evil story.

It should take the spiritual and philosophical concerns seriously.

The prose should favor clarity, precision, atmosphere, and emotional truth over flashy genre theatrics.

Tension should come from:

- moral drift
- loyalty
- ideology
- belonging
- fear
- identity
- the loss of nuance
- systems growing beyond human control

When writing scenes, preserve complexity. Let multiple sides make real sense. Avoid obvious sermonizing.

---

## Relationship to the First Novel

This novel is in the same universe as *What It Feels Like to Be You*, but it should stand independently.

Do not assume the reader has read the first book.

However, preserve continuity with the world, themes, technology, and timeline.

Important relationship:

- *What It Feels Like to Be You* explores the creation, public rollout, misuse, and eventual regulation of empathy technology.
- *Only I Can Feel Me* explores the resistance movement that forms in opposition to that technology.

The books should feel like two sides of the same philosophical argument.

One explores **shared feeling**.

The other explores **the defense of interiority**.

## Core Themes

All work on this project should remain aligned with these themes:

### 1. The sanctity of inner life
The movement believes human interiority is sacred and must not be machine-mediated.

### 2. Radicalization through belonging
People do not usually become extremists because of abstract ideas alone. They become radicalized through community, grief, purpose, and identity.

### 3. Ideology escaping its founders
Once HumansRHumans spreads online, it mutates beyond the founder's control.

### 4. Ends justify the means
HumansRHumans ultimately becomes wrong not because every concern is invalid, but because it adopts corrupt means in service of its cause.

### 5. Human systems escaping control
Technology escapes its inventors. Movements escape their founders. Ideas replicate beyond anyone's ability to contain them.

### 6. Spiritual seriousness
The Sangha origins matter. The movement should not merely borrow spiritual imagery. It should arise from sincere contemplative concerns that are later weaponized or distorted.

### 7. Regulation is not resolution
Even when society regulates empathy technology, the movement does not end. The ideology persists and intensifies in splintered forms.

---

## HumansRHumans Ideology

The AI should treat the ideology of HumansRHumans as intellectually serious, at least in its original form.

Core beliefs may include:

- the human mind is the last uncolonized domain
- empathy should be earned, not transmitted
- consciousness should not be machine-mediated
- emotional experience must remain sovereign
- technological empathy is a violation of the self
- direct access to another's emotional state crosses a line that should not be crossed

The movement should be compelling enough that a thoughtful person could be drawn in.

Over time, however, those ideas become simplified, hardened, memeified, and weaponized as the movement spreads online.

This mutation should be gradual and believable.

---

## Movement Structure

HumansRHumans should evolve in stages:

### Stage 1: Sangha origin
A contemplative or spiritually serious group concerned with presence, suffering, and human connection.

### Stage 2: Schism after tragedy
A traumatic event involving empathy technology fractures the community.

### Stage 3: Small underground movement
Private meetings, manifestos, secrecy, encrypted channels, and moral seriousness.

### Stage 4: Internet decentralization
The movement spreads online. New members join without the original philosophical depth or discipline.

### Stage 5: Splintering and extremism
Different cells interpret the ideology differently. Some stay nonviolent. Others escalate.

### Stage 6: Post-regulation persistence
Even after the original political goal is achieved, the movement continues and mutates.

The AI should preserve this lifecycle in planning and drafting.

## Things the AI Should Do

The AI should help with:

- outlining acts, chapters, and scenes
- brainstorming characters, factions, and backstory
- drafting prose in a literary and psychologically grounded style
- refining ideological language and internal documents
- generating worldbuilding notes
- building thematic consistency
- helping distinguish factions within HumansRHumans
- tracking timeline continuity with *What It Feels Like to Be You*
- creating notes files, character files, faction files, and scene plans
- identifying weak or shallow story logic
- challenging simplistic moral framing
- preserving ambiguity where appropriate

The AI should be willing to say when an idea feels too obvious, too generic, too on-the-nose, or too shallow.

---

## Things the AI Should Avoid

The AI must avoid:

- flattening HumansRHumans into generic villains
- treating the Sangha origins as fake or cynical from the start
- reducing the book to action-thriller cliches
- making every antagonist cruel, insane, or obviously wrong
- writing dialogue that sounds like exposition pretending to be conversation
- overexplaining the philosophy in stiff monologues
- turning the founder into a stock cult leader
- making the recruit stupid or naive in a shallow way
- overusing jargon
- losing the emotional realism of gradual radicalization
- forgetting that the movement's original goal is eventually achieved
- treating regulation as the end of the human problem

Do not default to spectacle when moral drift would be more interesting.

## Guidance for Drafting New Material

When drafting scenes or notes:

- Anchor the emotional stakes in lived experience
- Let ideology emerge through character, ritual, argument, and action
- Favor specific details over abstract generalities
- Make the movement feel attractive before making it frightening
- Preserve moral complexity
- Show how online decentralization strips context from original ideas
- Keep returning to the core tension: human interiority versus machine-mediated empathy

If writing movement documents, vary the voices:
- founder voice
- recruit perspective
- manifesto fragments
- splinter faction rhetoric
- leaked internal communications

This will help the project feel layered and alive.

## Final Principle

This book should not ask only:

**What if a resistance movement opposes empathy technology?**

It should ask:

**What happens when people try to protect humanity, and in doing so become willing to abandon the very human values they meant to defend?**

That question should remain at the heart of the project.
