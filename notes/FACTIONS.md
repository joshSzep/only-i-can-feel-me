# Factions

As HumansRHumans grows, it fragments into multiple factions.

---

## Originalists

Remain loyal to the founder’s philosophy.

Oppose violence.

Advocate regulation and ethical limits.

---

## Sabotage Faction

The first serious distortion of the founder's original vision.

They argue that empathy technology is an existential threat to human interiority.

Because the threat is existential, they believe damage is justified.

They do not always begin with fantasies of spectacle.

They begin with the logic of necessity.

If the technology cannot be persuaded, regulated, or restrained quickly enough, then it must be obstructed materially.

The first action that makes this faction impossible to dismiss is not spectacular violence.

It is intimidation and targeted harassment of researchers or clinicians associated with the technology.

This matters because it can still be rationalized as pressure rather than terror.

Property damage, disruption, intimidation, and strategic leaks become thinkable under this logic.

The founder opposes this faction's methods, but the faction can still quote the movement's deepest wound back at them.

Their key doctrinal move is to claim that the founder cared above all about interior sovereignty, and that true sovereignty allows no compromise.

From there they argue that restraint, regulation, and bounded clinical use are merely softer names for surrender.

By the late stage of the novel, a known splinter leader using the pseudonym **Second Bell** is the first person to voice the warning language against Kate Minform on the page.

That matters because the rhetoric no longer reads like anonymous overflow.

It arrives with recognizable authority.

Second Bell's channel presence should be austere rather than charismatic.

The account feels severe, disciplined, and stripped of ornament, which helps cruelty pass as moral clarity.

Second Bell often writes in numbered doctrinal posts, which gives escalation the look of principle being clarified step by step.

At the sentence level, those posts should feel coldly logical, making coercion sound like deduction.

This leader should be a later figure Leah mainly knows through the movement's channels rather than someone rooted in her earlier Sangha relationships.

On the page, this leader should remain known only as **Second Bell** rather than being unmasked into a civilian identity.

---

## Accelerationists

Believe empathy technology must be destroyed entirely.

View compromise as weakness.

---

## Purists

Argue that any technological mediation of consciousness is unacceptable.

Reject regulation as insufficient.

---

## Internet Cells

Decentralized groups with loose ideological alignment.

Often adopt simplified slogans without philosophical grounding.
