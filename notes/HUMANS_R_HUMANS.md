# HumansRHumans

HumansRHumans is a decentralized resistance movement opposing empathy technology.

Its core belief:

Human consciousness should never be machine-mediated.

---

## Origin

The movement emerges from a contemplative community after a tragic event involving empathy technology.

It forms **before** the public breakthrough and launch events at the opening of *What It Feels Like to Be You*.

Its earliest members are not reacting to headlines from the outside.

They are reacting to what one of their own saw from inside the development process.

A respected Sangha member participates in early Empathy Engine calibration and warns that the true danger is not only transmission, but retention.

If feeling can be captured, cataloged, and reused, then interior life becomes infrastructure.

When that member's own recorded emotional material is kept and reused after assurances of ephemerality, the Sangha experiences it as the first unforgivable crossing of the line.

When the member dies not long afterward, the harm stops being arguable.

The death itself is not caused by the technology.

It is a random mugging gone wrong.

That senselessness matters.

It means the movement's founding grief is morally tangled from the beginning.

Early members know the truth.

Publicly, however, they allow the death to blur into the movement's indictment of the technology.

They do not always tell a direct lie.

They allow implication, grief, and rhetoric to make the story cleaner than it really is.

The death becomes the movement's deepest wound and a source of grief, guilt, and myth from the very beginning.

HumansRHumans begins as a response to that betrayal.

Initially the movement seeks:

- public awareness
- ethical debate
- technological restraint

It also seeks to expose the hidden assumption already built into the technology:

that interior states can become detachable objects.

---

## Structure

The movement begins small and organized.

Over time it becomes decentralized through online communication.

Cells operate independently.

Leadership becomes diffuse.

---

## Ideological Drift

The movement's first simplification begins at the beginning.

Its founding wound is already being turned into a cleaner public symbol than reality permits.

As the movement spreads online:

- slogans replace philosophy
- ideas become simplified
- factions diverge

The earliest serious distortion comes from a sabotage current that argues empathy technology is an existential threat and therefore damage is justified.

Its first decisive action is intimidation and targeted harassment of researchers or clinicians tied to the technology.

Some cells adopt extreme interpretations.

The movement gradually loses ideological coherence.
