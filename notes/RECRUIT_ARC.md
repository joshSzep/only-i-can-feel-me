# Recruit Arc

The recruit is the emotional center of *Only I Can Feel Me*.

The novel is fundamentally the story of their **gradual radicalization and eventual disillusionment**.

The reader should experience the movement through the recruit's perspective.

The recruit must be sympathetic and psychologically believable.

They should not begin as an extremist.

They should begin as someone searching for something meaningful.

---

# Stage 1 — Disorientation

The recruit begins in a period of instability.

Possible factors include:

- loneliness
- loss
- grief
- disillusionment with modern society
- confusion about technology's role in human life
- desire for meaning or belonging

They feel disconnected from the world.

They are searching for something grounded and authentic.

---

# Stage 2 — Discovery

The recruit encounters the Sangha community.

The atmosphere is calm, thoughtful, contemplative.

Members speak about:

- awareness
- suffering
- attention
- compassion
- the nature of mind

For the first time in a long while, the recruit feels:

- seen
- grounded
- welcomed

The Sangha becomes a place of refuge.

---

# Stage 3 — Philosophical Awakening

The debates about empathy technology begin.

Members discuss questions like:

- should machines mediate human emotional experience?
- is empathy something that should be technologically transmitted?
- does this violate the boundary of consciousness?

The recruit becomes fascinated by these discussions.

The HumansRHumans philosophy feels intellectually serious and morally important.

---

# Stage 4 — The Fracture

The tragic event involving empathy technology occurs.

This event emotionally shocks the community.

Some members believe the technology must now be actively resisted.

The recruit sympathizes with this position.

HumansRHumans begins to form.

The recruit joins.

---

# Stage 5 — Belonging

The movement provides the recruit with something powerful:

purpose.

They feel part of something meaningful.

They begin participating in:

- meetings
- organizing
- messaging
- outreach
- online communication

Their identity becomes increasingly tied to the movement.

---

# Stage 6 — Escalation

As the movement grows online, new factions emerge.

Some members advocate increasingly extreme tactics.

At first the recruit rationalizes these developments.

They believe:

the cause is too important to fail.

The recruit begins accepting compromises they once would have rejected.

---

# Stage 7 — Doubt

The recruit begins noticing:

- ideological simplification
- growing hostility
- disregard for nuance
- rhetoric replacing philosophy

They begin questioning whether the movement still resembles its original ideals.

But leaving would mean losing:

- community
- purpose
- identity

So they stay.

---

# Stage 8 — Confrontation

The recruit eventually speaks with the founder.

The founder expresses concern about the direction the movement has taken.

The recruit realizes the founder no longer fully controls the movement.

This conversation plants the seed of clarity.

---

# Stage 9 — The Political Victory

Empathy technology becomes regulated.

The movement's original goal has effectively been achieved.

The recruit expects celebration or dissolution.

Instead the movement intensifies.

Some factions now argue the technology must be eliminated entirely.

---

# Stage 10 — Realization

The recruit finally understands.

The movement is no longer fighting for its original goal.

It is sustaining itself.

The ideology has become self-perpetuating.

The recruit recognizes the tragic truth:

The movement meant to defend humanity has lost the humanity it was meant to protect.

---

# Final Emotional State

The recruit does not end as a triumphant hero.

They end with clarity.

They understand:

- why the movement began
- why people joined
- why it changed
- why it will likely continue

Their final realization is both sobering and deeply human.
