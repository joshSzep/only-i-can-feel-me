# Premise

**Only I Can Feel Me** is a philosophical science-fiction novel set in the same universe as *What It Feels Like to Be You*.

The story explores the rise, evolution, and fragmentation of an underground movement called **HumansRHumans**, a group that opposes any technological breach of the boundary between human consciousness and machines.

The novel follows a **new recruit** who gradually becomes radicalized through belonging, ideology, and purpose.

The movement originates within a **Sangha-like contemplative community** concerned about the ethical implications of empathy technology. After a tragic event involving the technology, part of the community breaks away and forms HumansRHumans.

What begins as a sincere philosophical resistance slowly evolves into a decentralized global movement.

Over time:

- the ideology spreads online
- new members join without understanding the original philosophy
- factions develop competing interpretations
- tactics escalate

By the time empathy technology becomes regulated, the movement's original political goal has effectively been achieved.

But the movement does not stop.

HumansRHumans has grown beyond its founders, beyond its purpose, and beyond anyone's control.

The novel explores the recruit’s realization that the movement meant to protect humanity may have become something far more dangerous.
