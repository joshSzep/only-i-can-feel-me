# World

The novel takes place in the same near-future world as *What It Feels Like to Be You*.

The defining technological development of the era is **empathy technology** — a system that allows a person to experience the emotional state of another.

This technology has transformative implications for:

- psychology
- criminal justice
- diplomacy
- relationships
- therapy
- education

It also introduces profound ethical concerns.

---

## Social Reaction

Humanity is divided in its response to empathy technology.

Some view it as a revolutionary tool for compassion.

Others view it as a violation of the boundary between individuals.

Public debate becomes intense.

Political movements emerge both supporting and opposing the technology.

---

## Cultural Context

Several cultural forces shape the world of the novel:

- rapid technological advancement
- social media amplification
- decentralized online movements
- rising distrust of institutions
- renewed interest in contemplative traditions

HumansRHumans emerges at the intersection of these forces.

---

## Political Outcome

By the conclusion of *What It Feels Like to Be You*, empathy technology becomes **regulated**.

Regulation restricts how the technology can be used.

However, the existence of the technology itself cannot be undone.

The world must now live with it.
